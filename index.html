Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I expect during my first appointment?

Your first appointment will typically include a comprehensive medical history review, physical examination, and discussion of your symptoms and concerns. We may also perform or schedule diagnostic tests as needed. I'll take time to answer your questions and develop an initial treatment plan or further evaluation strategy.

How often should I have a heart check-up?

The frequency of heart check-ups depends on your age, risk factors, and existing conditions. Generally, adults with no known heart issues should have a cardiovascular risk assessment every 4-6 years. Those with risk factors or existing heart conditions may need more frequent evaluations, ranging from every 3-12 months depending on their specific situation.

Do you accept insurance?

Yes, I accept most major insurance plans, including Medicare and private insurance. Our office staff will verify your coverage before your appointment and can help explain any potential out-of-pocket costs. If you have questions about specific insurance plans, please contact our office directly.

What are the warning signs of heart problems I should watch for?

Important warning signs include chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, heart palpitations (irregular heartbeat), unusual fatigue, dizziness or lightheadedness, swelling in the legs or ankles, and fainting. Women may experience less typical symptoms such as nausea, back or jaw pain. If you experience these symptoms, especially chest pain, seek medical attention immediately.

How can I prepare for my appointment?

To prepare for your appointment, bring a list of your current medications (including over-the-counter drugs and supplements), your medical records or test results from other providers, a list of your symptoms and when they occur, and your family medical history. It's also helpful to write down any questions you have so we can address all your concerns during the visit.
